    Comments/discussion

    For current or past members of the ITLC - we have a new cohort of instructor trainers about to join us. What advice would you give them to make the most of their training?

    Do the reading and try to make sure that they have 20+ minutes of space in between workday and training session (we realize that can be challenging). Try to make space in life for the training, doing it right after work was challenging - needed more time/space.

    Take this as a learning opportunity and a fun learning activity. When doing the training, sometimes felt fear of contributing - there was a lot of active chatting and talking, and sometimes you can get the feeling that you are supposed to already know everything. Would have been good advice to take it as fun, get to know people, troubleshoot together rather than trying to showcase what you already know.

    Impressed by the trainer training with how invested everyone was in it.  It's more fun when everyone is involved, invested. Importance of interacting with other people in the cohort and thinking about continuity of the trainer community. You will be working with each other.

    Taking the training made me a better teacher and communicator.

    What helped me the most was being a maintainer, especially if you can be second or third maintainer behind someone who has been doing it a while. Reinforces values and pedagogy. 

    In the time you are carving out around working and training, budget time for the book because it is worth a close read and is easier to read when you have a reason to dig in. Use Slack during training to keep in touch and to get to know all the trainers in your cohort. 

    Question about time commitment - how much time should people expect to put in to being on the ITLC per month?

    Some async work, such as being on Slack to approve pull requests to update meeting minutes, etc.

    Committee has the normal issues of anything like this. It is monthly, and can be slow and then can pick up depending on the schedule. Flexibility for making time. But for example as the monthly trainer meeting approaches, making sure that we are on top of things. Can be ~2.5 to 3 or 4 hours per month. 

    It does go in waves - there are busy and light times. For example trainer applications are coming up and that will require some time. 

    Reliable 2-3 hours per month, with lots of support from Sher and the Core Team. Easy to feel that you're not putting in as much time as needed, but it is a supportive group.

    Core Team relies heavily on this group - gratitude from Sher for all the ITLC contributes to the community. Meetings are often a lot of conversation, not busy work. Sher leans in on the group to understand and guide the direction of the trainer community.
